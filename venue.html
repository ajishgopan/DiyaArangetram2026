About the Temple

A Sacred Space for a Sacred Occasion

Sri Guruvaayoorappan Temple in Morganville, New Jersey is one of the most beloved Hindu temples in the tri-state area — a beacon of spiritual tranquility and cultural richness dedicated to Lord Krishna, revered here as Guruvaayoorappan.

The temple's architecture reflects the vibrant traditions of South Indian temple design, with a beautifully crafted Gopuram and stunning interior shrines housing Guruvaayoorappan, Ekambareswara, Sivasubrahmanya, Ram Parivar, and Ganesh. Visitors consistently describe it as a faithful rendition of the sacred Guruvayur Temple in Kerala — a rare spiritual sanctuary that feels authentically Indian in the heart of New Jersey.

It is deeply fitting that Diya's Arangetram takes place here — in a space where the divine is present in every corner, and where the art of Bharatanatyam finds its truest and most sacred home.

What to Know

Visitor Information

Parking
Ample free parking is available on the temple grounds. The temple has a vast area with dedicated parking facilities for all visitors.
Dress Code
Traditional or formal attire is encouraged as a mark of respect for the sacred space. Please dress modestly — the temple is an active place of worship.
Footwear
As is customary in all Hindu temples, footwear must be removed before entering the temple premises. Dedicated areas are provided.
Photography
Photography during the performance is subject to the temple's guidelines. Please be considerate of other guests and the sanctity of the occasion.
Dining
The temple houses Sri Krishna Bhojnalya, serving delicious South Indian vegan cuisine. Lunch will be served as part of the event at 12:00 Noon.
Deities
Shrines for Guruvaayoorappan (Lord Krishna), Ekambareswara, Sivasubrahmanya, Ram Parivar, and Ganesh.
